AR Snow Goggles

  • Overview
    • AR Snow Goggles is a hardware project building augmented-reality ski and snowboard goggles to improve on-moutain visibility on the low visibility days.
    • Inspired and motivated by my own experiences in the backcountry.
    • Currently in active development. Claude is helping immensely with the design and development.
  • Problem
    • I wanted to chase powder, but the visibility was too low due to the storm clouds overhead. The best terrain is in the backcoutnry, but it's also the most dangerous.
    • The combination of low visibility and dangerout terrain is very high risk and high consequence if you make a mistake.
    • The goggles boost brightness, contrast, and overall visbility so you can see the terrain features in front of you and improve situational awareness - minimizing risk.
    • Highlights gradients in sloping terrain and edges of cliffs and cliffsides.
  • BOM
    • GPU: NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano Developer Kit
    • Camera: Arducam xISP - Darksee, Pre-tuned ISP, 2MP Low Light MIPI Camera
    • Display: 0.49" Micro OLED Display 1920x1080 3000 NITS
    • Combiner / Beamsplitter: 25 x 25mm 30R/70T, VIS Plate Beamsplitter
  • Design
    • Light enters the camera, processed by the GPU, output to the mini-OLED display, directed to the eyes via the beamsplitter and combiner.
    • CLAHE is used to enhance the contrast of the image from the camera.
